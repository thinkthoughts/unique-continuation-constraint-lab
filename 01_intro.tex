\section{Introduction}

Unique continuation asks when local smallness or vanishing forces global structure.
For Schr\"odinger equations, a central result states that sufficiently strong decay at two distinct times can force the solution to be identically zero.

This note is an expository bridge. It does not propose new mathematics. It organizes the standard proof logic as a constraint pipeline:
\[
\text{two-time decay}
\to \text{Carleman weight}
\to \text{Hardy-type inequality}
\to \text{contradiction}
\to \text{zero solution}.
\]
