\begin{abstract}
We present an expository, constraint-based view of unique continuation results for Schrödinger equations.
The standard proof structure—two-time decay, Carleman estimates, Hardy-type inequalities, and contradiction—is preserved.
Our contribution is organizational: a reproducible pipeline that clarifies how these components interact to force the zero solution.
\end{abstract}
