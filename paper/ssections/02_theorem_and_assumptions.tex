\section{Theorem and assumptions}

A simplified model is
\[
i\partial_t u = -\Delta u + V(x)u.
\]

A classical unique-continuation statement has the following form: if the solution satisfies sufficiently strong two-time decay,
\[
|u(x,t_1)|, |u(x,t_2)| \le C e^{-a|x|^2},
\]
for \(a\) sufficiently large under appropriate hypotheses, then
\[
u(x,t) \equiv 0.
\]

The exact hypotheses depend on the model, potential, coefficients, dimension, and function spaces.
