\section{Carleman weights}

Carleman estimates introduce exponential weights that convert pointwise decay into global constraints.

A typical transformation has the form
\[
v(x,t) = e^{\phi(x,t)} u(x,t),
\]
where \(\phi\) is a suitable weight function.

Carleman inequalities then yield estimates of the type
\[
\| e^{\phi} u \| \le C \| e^{\phi} (i\partial_t + \Delta - V) u \|.
\]

In the context of unique continuation, this step amplifies decay assumptions so that smallness must act under the constraints imposed by the equation.
