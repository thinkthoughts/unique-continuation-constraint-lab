\section{Hardy-type inequalities}

Hardy-type inequalities constrain how concentrated a function can be relative to its variation.

A model inequality is
\[
\int_{\mathbb{R}^n} \frac{|u(x)|^2}{|x|^2}\,dx
\le C \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} |\nabla u(x)|^2\,dx.
\]

In the pipeline, such inequalities enforce that strong spatial concentration requires a corresponding variation cost.

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Interpretation.}
Concentration must pay variation cost; otherwise, constraints are violated.

\medskip

This prevents a non-zero solution from remaining highly localized without violating derivative bounds.
