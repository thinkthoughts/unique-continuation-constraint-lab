\section{Hardy-type inequalities}

Hardy-type inequalities relate concentration and variation. A canonical model inequality is
\[
\int_{\mathbb{R}^n} \frac{|u(x)|^2}{|x|^2}\,dx
\le C \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} |\nabla u(x)|^2\,dx.
\]

In the pipeline, concentration pays variation cost. This step prevents an over-concentrated non-zero solution from satisfying the weighted constraints without producing a large derivative cost.
